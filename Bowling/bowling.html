Explainer

Bowling balls have a multitude of factors that effect how the ball performs, here is a quick guide to bowling ball reaction.

Cover Types
There are 3 main types of covers for a bowling ball: Plastic, Urethane, and Reactive Resin.
  • Plastic balls are designed to go straight. Most bowlers use them to hit spares.
  • Urethane is an older technology that has resurfaced in the past decade or so. It hooks less than Reactive, but is more controllable and predictable.
  • Most bowling balls produced today are reactive bowling balls. They provide the most hook potential.
Core Numbers
Inside each bowling ball is a core. These cores have different numbers given to them depending on what it does as it rolls. These numbers help bowlers determine what the ball will do for them.
  • Radius of Gyration (RG): RG determines how soon the ball begins to rev and change direction. It ranges from 2.45-2.80. The lower the RG, the sooner the ball revs and the earlier it changes direction
  • Differential (Diff): Diff determines the bowling ball's "flare potential." As a bowling ball rotates around it's core, fresh bowling ball surface touches the lane, creating a "flare" of oil on the bowling ball. Diff-ranging from 0.001-0.060 determines how wide that track is, therefore telling you how much fresh cover touches the lane when you throw it. The higher the diff, the more the ball will generally hook.
