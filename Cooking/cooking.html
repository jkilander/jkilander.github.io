This summer, I've been looking to expand some of my cooking skills. I have seeked out recipes for some of my favorite foods to try and make them myself. Cooking is a very rewarding activity that I have really enjoyed! Here is a few things I've been making recently.

Steak Sandwiches

I have always loved a Philly Cheesesteak. I attempted to make my own using This recipe.

Some things that went well with this recipe were that the steak, onions and peppers came out really flavorful and tender. I did them in the same cast iron skillet and they were delicious.

Some things I would do differently with this recipe is to use some kind of cheese sauce to pour over it instead of using slices of cheese. I also forgot the bread in the oven and it got a little burnt.

Lobster Tails

I got two lobster tails from work for $10. I had never made these before and followed This recipe's method to cooking them.

These looked and tasted amazing. I used the butter sauce from the recipe and it complimented the sweetness of the lobster tails perfectly. I had it with a side of rice.

When I originally took them out of the oven, they were a little undercooked and gummy. I put them back in for another 2 minutes and they were much better.

Chicken Cutlets

I got on a bit of a sandwich kick. This was my attempt to create a chicken cutlet sandwich similar to one I had at Casetta. I followed this recipe but substituted the kale for baby arugula, and provolone for fresh mozzarella.

This is the meal I am most proud of. The chicken was nice and crispy, the bread had a nice crunch, the greens and tomato were perfect compliments to the sandwich. We had twice-baked potatoes on the side because my girlfriend loves them. The only thing I would've done differently is to season the bread crumbs that I breaded the chicken with.
